Women's Safety Situation Around the World

Women of all ages and background are facing unsafe situations constantly and equally. In some countries, women's situation is much safer than in others. Down below are the reasons that cause women in dangers

1. A Culture of Violence Against Women

- Almost all the societies in the world don't put women on priority and are not friendly enough to women. The patriarchal society is deeply ingrained in various cultures. This kind of society plants the seeds of gender-based inequality, discrimination, and violence

- Male gaze in pop culture nowadays is very common. Sexualizing women figures in games and misogyny advertisement can be seen everywhere

2. Examples of Sexual Violence

- Sexual assault: Any sexual act where the victim is threatened, forced, coerced, and/or exploited, or any sexual act that is violent and/or unwanted or not consented to by the victim

- Rape: The penetration, no matter how slight, of the vagina or anus with any body part or object, or oral penetration by a sex organ of another person, without the consent of the victim.

- Group Rape: Sometimes referred to as “gang rape”. The rape of one person by multiple people in a group.

- Forced Sexual Contact: Any incident where sexual contact is forced upon a nonconsenting victim. This included the intentional touching of another person’s sexual organs.

- Sexual Torture: sexual violence that is used to inflict severe pain/suffering on the victim, to punish the victim, or to coerce them into doing or saying something.

- Child Sexual Abuse: Coercion of a minor or young person to take part in sexual activities. Whether the child is aware of what's happening or not does not matter.

- Incest: Sexual relations between closely related family members.

- Sexual Harassment: Unwanted behavior of a sexual nature that makes the victim feel degraded, intimidated, humiliated, or hostile/offensive. Can often occur in workplaces or in public.

- Unwanted Sexual Advances: A form of sexual harassment. Any unwelcome request/pressure for sexual activities, favors, or verbal/physical sexual communications. Can be expressed implicitly or explicitly as a term of the victim’s employment, when committed in the workplace.
